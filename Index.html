The Science Behind Spanfull

Why We Focus on These Four Metrics

Spanfull's assessment focuses on cardio fitness, muscular strength, balance and sleep because decades of research show these are the most predictive biomarkers of long-term health and longevity. Each measure in our assessment meets three criteria:

  • Validated in peer-reviewed studies with large cohorts
  • Practical to perform accurately at home with minimal equipment
  • Directly actionable through lifestyle changes

Cardio Fitness (VO₂-max)

A 2018 JAMA study of 122,007 patients found those with high cardiorespiratory fitness had 80% lower mortality risk. Our at-home VO₂-max estimation correlates strongly (r=0.92) with lab tests.

Grip Strength

The Prospective Urban-Rural Epidemiology (PURE) study showed each 5 kg decrease in grip strength associated with 16% higher cardiovascular mortality and 17% higher all-cause mortality.

Balance

A 2022 British Journal of Sports Medicine study found inability to complete a 10-second one-leg stand was associated with 84% higher 10-year all-cause mortality in middle-aged and older adults.

Sleep Quality

Research shows poor Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index scores correlate with higher risks of hypertension, diabetes, and depression. Improving sleep quality can rapidly enhance multiple health markers.
